Wine Bars

Cafayate's wine bars are the perfect place to sample the region's wines without committing to a full winery visit. Whether you're looking for a guided tasting flight, a glass with a picada, or a full dinner paired with extreme-altitude wines, these are the spots to know.

Bad Brothers Wine & Food Experience

Half a block from the main plaza in a beautifully restored old colonial house, Bad Brothers is Cafayate's most complete wine and dining experience. They offer the only dedicated extreme-altitude wine tastings in town, featuring wines from vineyards at 1,700 to 2,680 meters. The internationally-inspired menu ranges from shared plates and picadas to Argentine parrilla. Book a personalized wine blending session with their in-house sommelier for a truly hands-on experience. The garden terrace with live music makes it a great evening destination.

Bodega Amalaya Wine Bar

Set against stunning views of Cerro San Isidro, Amalaya's wine bar combines regional cuisine designed for sharing with guided tastings of their six exclusive varietals — wines you can only try here. Choose from classic or extended tasting flights, or try their hands-on wine blending experience where you create your own blend. Reservations required.

amalaya.com →

Chato's Wine Bar

A Cafayate institution since 2008, this intimate wine bar on San Martín 223 is run by Eduardo "Chato" Cruz, a passionate and knowledgeable English-speaking host. The beautiful custom wine barrel tables are handcrafted by Chato himself. Order a five-wine tasting flight with a traditional picada and let Eduardo guide you through the region's best whites, rosés, and reds. Open daily from 7 PM.

At the wineries

Many wineries have their own tasting rooms where you can sample and buy wines directly. Prices are often more accessible than in downtown wine bars, and you get the full vineyard experience. See our Tastings guide for recommended wineries to visit.

Wine ice cream

Don't leave Cafayate without trying the famous wine ice cream. A unique regional specialty that combines the flavors of Torrontés and Malbec with the freshness of artisanal gelato. You'll find it at heladerías around the main plaza.
