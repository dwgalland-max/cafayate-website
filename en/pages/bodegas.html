Wineries of Cafayate

Cafayate offers an accessible and welcoming wine tasting experience. Most wineries are located in the town itself or in its surroundings, making it easy to visit them on foot or by bicycle. Between downtown wine bars and tasting rooms at the wineries themselves, there are options for every palate.

Tastings & Wine Bars

Cafayate has wine bars in the center of town and tasting rooms at the wineries themselves. The wine bars are perfect for sampling the region's wines without committing to a full winery visit, while the bodegas offer the complete vineyard experience.

Bad Brothers Wine & Food Experience

Half a block from the main plaza in a beautifully restored old colonial house, Bad Brothers is Cafayate's most complete wine and dining experience. They offer the only dedicated extreme-altitude wine tastings in town, featuring wines from vineyards at 1,700 to 2,680 meters. The internationally-inspired menu ranges from shared plates and picadas to Argentine parrilla. Book a personalized wine blending session with their in-house sommelier for a truly hands-on experience. The garden terrace with live music makes it a great evening destination.

Bodega Amalaya Wine Bar

Set against stunning views of Cerro San Isidro, Amalaya's wine bar combines regional cuisine designed for sharing with guided tastings of their six exclusive varietals — wines you can only try here. Choose from classic or extended tasting flights, or try their hands-on wine blending experience where you create your own blend. Reservations required.

amalaya.com →

Chato's Wine Bar

A Cafayate institution since 2008, this intimate wine bar on San Martín 223 is run by Eduardo "Chato" Cruz, a passionate and knowledgeable English-speaking host. The beautiful custom wine barrel tables are handcrafted by Chato himself. Order a five-wine tasting flight with a traditional picada and let Eduardo guide you through the region's best whites, rosés, and reds. Open daily from 7 PM.

Recommended Wineries

These are our recommended wineries for tastings:

  • Bodega Amalaya — Six exclusive varietals, wine blending experience, and stunning views of Cerro San Isidro
  • Bad Brothers Wine & Food Experience — Extreme altitude wines and dining in the heart of Cafayate
  • Dal Borgo — Traditional winery with tastings in Cafayate
  • Domingo Hermanos — Pioneers in Cafayate with an outstanding line of premium wines
  • Bodega El Esteco — Tradition since 1892 with historic architecture in the heart of Cafayate
  • Finca las Nubes — On the outskirts of Cafayate, with spectacular views
  • Bodega Nanni — Family winery with over 120 years of tradition
  • Bodega Piattelli Vineyards — Panoramic views and excellent Torrontés
  • El Porvenir de Cafayate — Laborum line and dining experience at La Pausa
  • Bodega Tierra Colorada — At the entrance to Cafayate, ideal for tastings
  • Bodega Vasija Secreta — One of Cafayate's oldest wineries, in downtown Cafayate
  • Viñas en Flor — Single-varietal wines with a gastronomic experience at their restaurant
  • Bodega Yacochuya — Legendary vineyard at 2,035 m.a.s.l., cultivated by Marcos Etchart

More Wineries in the Calchaquí Valleys

In addition to our recommendations, these wineries are also part of the wine region:

  • Agustin Lanus Wines (SUNAL) — Award-winning SUNAL extreme altitude wines, vineyards at 2,680 m.a.s.l.
  • Bodega Altupalka — Extreme altitude vineyards at 2,500 m.a.s.l.
  • Bodega Animaná — Winery in the town of Animaná, near Cafayate
  • Bodega Colomé — Record-altitude vineyards, including Altura Máxima at 3,111 m.a.s.l.
  • Bodega Domingo Molina — Dedicated to premium wines, operated by the Domingo family
  • Bodega El Tránsito — Winery in the town of Cafayate
  • Bodega Felix Lavaque — Family winery in Cafayate
  • Bodega Humanao — Extreme altitude winery with vineyards at 2,600 m.a.s.l.
  • Finca Quara — Quality wines from the Calchaquí Valley with great value
  • Bodega San Pedro de Yacochuya — Vineyards in the Yacochuya area at 2,035 m.a.s.l.
  • Stutz Vinos — Organic certified since 2015, variety of unique wines
  • Bodega Tacuil — Vineyard at 2,600 m.a.s.l., founded by Raúl Dávalos
  • Bodega Tukma — Vineyards at 2,000 m.a.s.l. in the Calchaquí Valleys

Tasting tips

Most wineries offer tastings with or without prior reservation. Prices are affordable and many also offer pairings with local cheeses and empanadas. We recommend visiting 2 to 3 wineries per day to fully enjoy each experience.
